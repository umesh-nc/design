My recommendation

Lead with The Constellation. Second-choice Keystone A.

The Constellation is the only mark that says what the product actually does. Every competitor in donor management looks like an enterprise CRM logo — generic monograms, abstract shapes, conservative type. ATLAS has a chance to plant a flag with a mark that's about the relationships between donors, not the database that stores them. The gold "closed-won" star is a useful brand asset across the product: campaign celebrations, milestone moments, the EOD wrap-up card on Tasks.

The Keystone A is the right backup. It carries gravitas on a board deck and a cathedral lobby alike, scales cleanly to 16px, and the gold keystone gives you a tasteful accent without leaning Catholic-specific. Use it if the constellation reads too playful to the bishop.

All four are workable. Take them into a 20-minute review with three people — the constellation will either land immediately or get ruled out. That's the signal.
